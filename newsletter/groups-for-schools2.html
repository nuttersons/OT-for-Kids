Occupational Therapy Groups in Schools

OT for Kids provide Occupational Therapy groups that improve the vital skills needed for growth and development in schools across North West.

Groups we offer

We provide group therapy in schools. One of the many benefits of providing treatment in groups rather than individually is that groups enable a higher number of children to receive high quality therapy at one time.

All of our groups are designed to further the development of each child across one or more of the following key skill areas within school; handwriting, concentration, movement and fine motor skills.

Group therapy is a term used to describe treatment that is completed in group sessions rather than on a one to one basis.

The main aim of therapy during all of our Occupational Therapy groups in school is to improve function. This is done through guided activities, therapist intervention and education regarding correct techniques for the task.

The Occupational Therapy groups we offer are:

Handwriting group

A group designed at improving pencil grip, visual perception, letter formation, legibility and spacing of writing for children who are having difficulty in producing clear writing, are slow at writing or are in pain when writing.

Fine motor group

A group for children who are having difficulty cutting, tying shoe laces, writing and dressing or participating in fine motor based games (Jenga, coin slot etc). Activities aim at strengthening both the muscles in the hand and the co-ordination of fine movements of the fingers and hand when completing activities.

Concentration group

A group designed to help those children who are easily distracted, having difficulty following instructions, maintaining sustained attention or difficulty following social rules such as taking turns or making friends.

Movement group

A group for children who have difficulty co-ordinating larger movements of the body. Children suitable for this group find the following tasks difficult:

  • Jumping & moving
  • Balancing
  • Throwing & catching
  • P.E & sports.

Please note: Maximum of 4 groups per school day.

OT for Kids work alongside schools in providing the best environment for children to learn, grow and develop. We work with a number of schools currently, including supporting children on a one to one basis and working in small groups with the Special Educational Needs Coordinator (SENCO). Our groups have been specifically designed for children who struggle with handwriting, concentrating in class, P.E or sport lessons (movement) and difficulty dressing or using scissors (fine motor skills).
